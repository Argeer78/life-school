# Community Covenant

Welcome to the Life School community.

Life School exists to give every human being the opportunity to better understand themselves and continue learning throughout life while respecting their dignity, freedom, and responsibility.

This covenant describes how we choose to work together while serving that Purpose.

It is not a list of punishments.

It is a shared commitment.

---

# Our Shared Commitment

We recognize that every person is capable of learning.

We therefore choose to treat one another with dignity, respect, honesty, and intellectual humility.

We are united by a common Purpose rather than by identical opinions.

---

# We Seek Understanding

We value understanding more than winning arguments.

Questions are welcomed.

Disagreement is welcomed.

Honest examination is welcomed.

Personal attacks are not.

---

# We Challenge Ideas

Life School encourages thoughtful criticism.

We examine ideas rigorously because improvement depends upon examination.

When challenging an idea, we seek to strengthen understanding rather than diminish the person presenting it.

---

# We Remain Humble

No contributor possesses complete understanding.

Every contribution remains open to examination.

Changing one's mind in light of better understanding is considered responsible stewardship rather than weakness.

---

# We Respect Human Dignity

Every person shall be treated with dignity regardless of:

- age,
- background,
- nationality,
- culture,
- profession,
- education,
- beliefs,
- level of experience.

Disagreement never diminishes a person's worth.

---

# We Protect Freedom

Participation in Life School is voluntary.

People remain free to contribute, question, disagree, pause, leave, and return.

Learning cannot be forced.

---

# We Accept Responsibility

Every contributor remains responsible for their own words, actions, and decisions.

We strive to communicate honestly, acknowledge mistakes, and correct them when necessary.

---

# We Prefer Clarity

We aim to write and speak clearly.

Complexity should exist only when necessary.

Whenever possible, we choose understanding over impressive language.

---

# We Welcome Improvement

Life School is never considered complete.

Every generation is encouraged to improve what previous generations began while remaining faithful to the Constitution.

Faithfulness to the Purpose is more important than attachment to existing work.

---

# Resolving Disagreements

When disagreements arise, we begin by returning to Constitutional Anchor CA-001.

Rather than asking:

"Who is right?"

we first ask:

"What most faithfully serves the Purpose of Life School?"

The Constitution remains our common point of reference.

---

# Closing Commitment

We do not expect perfection from ourselves or from one another.

We ask only that each person sincerely seeks to learn, contributes honestly, and treats others with dignity, freedom, and responsibility.

That is the spirit in which Life School grows.
