# Contributing to Life School

Welcome.

Thank you for considering contributing to Life School.

Life School is an institution dedicated to giving every human being the opportunity to better understand themselves and continue learning throughout life while respecting their dignity, freedom, and responsibility.

Every contribution should strengthen that Purpose.

Before contributing, please read the Foundation documents, beginning with:

1. CA-001 — The Purpose
2. LS-000 — The Manifesto
3. LS-001 — What is Life School?

These documents define the constitutional identity of Life School.

Everything else derives from them.

---

# Our Philosophy

Life School grows through learning.

It does not grow through accumulating ideas.

Every contribution should make the institution clearer, more faithful to its Purpose, or more helpful to learners.

A contribution is valuable because it strengthens the Purpose, not because it is new.

---

# Before You Contribute

Before writing code, documentation, research, educational material, or proposing a new feature, ask yourself:

- Does this help Life School fulfill its Purpose?
- Does this respect human dignity, freedom, and responsibility?
- Does this strengthen lifelong learning or self-understanding?
- Can it be explained more clearly?
- Is it necessary?

If the answer to these questions is unclear, continue examining the idea before proposing it.

---

# Contributions We Welcome

Life School welcomes thoughtful contributions, including:

- questions,
- corrections,
- research,
- editorial improvements,
- educational material,
- software,
- translations,
- accessibility improvements,
- documentation,
- constitutional proposals,
- constructive criticism.

Understanding may emerge from anyone.

---

# Constitutional First

Life School follows a simple principle:

Purpose before implementation.

Constitution before software.

Understanding before features.

If a proposal conflicts with the Constitution, the Constitution prevails.

---

# The Contribution Process

Every significant contribution follows the same process:

1. Identify the opportunity.
2. Explain the reasoning.
3. Relate it to the Purpose.
4. Challenge the proposal.
5. Improve it.
6. Submit it for review.
7. Document the decision.

The goal is not simply to add new work.

The goal is to strengthen Life School.

---

# Editorial Principles

When writing documentation or educational material:

- prefer clarity over complexity,
- prefer understanding over impressive language,
- prefer simplicity over unnecessary detail,
- distinguish facts from assumptions,
- acknowledge uncertainty honestly.

Every sentence should earn its place.

---

# Software Contributions

Software exists to serve the Constitution.

Before implementing a feature, identify:

- which constitutional document it derives from,
- which Purpose it strengthens,
- how it respects human dignity, freedom, and responsibility.

Features without constitutional justification should not be implemented.

---

# Disagreement

Disagreement is a normal part of learning.

Challenge ideas respectfully.

Question assumptions honestly.

Critique proposals rather than people.

The objective is deeper understanding, not winning arguments.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Life School is not considered complete.

Every contribution is part of an ongoing process of learning.

Future contributors are encouraged to improve today's work while remaining faithful to the Constitution.

Faithfulness to the Purpose is more important than loyalty to previous decisions.

---

# A Final Thought

Life School belongs to no single person.

It is entrusted to every generation that faithfully carries its Purpose forward.

If your contribution strengthens that Purpose, thank you.

Welcome to Life School.
