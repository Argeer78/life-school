# Greek Localization Style Guide

## 1. Purpose

Greek Lifeschool content should read as native Greek educational language, not
as a literal translation from English.

Translation must preserve the meaning, limits, and constitutional intent of
the source while using wording that a Greek speaker would naturally expect.
Clarity takes priority over structural similarity to the English text.

## 2. Voice

Greek Lifeschool language should be:

- direct;
- calm;
- clean;
- free from motivational exaggeration;
- free from a therapist tone;
- free from unnecessary formality;
- free from “extra sauce.”

The voice should respect the learner without flattering, diagnosing, lecturing,
or taking over their judgment.

## 3. Product Names

- Steward remains **Steward**.
- Lifeschool remains **Lifeschool**.

Product names are not translated or transliterated.

| Judgment | Κρίση |
| Responsibility | Ευθύνη |
| Freedom | Ελευθερία |
| Assumption | Υπόθεση |
| Interpretation | Ερμηνεία |
| Observation | Παρατήρηση |

| Boundary | Όριο |
| Decision | Απόφαση |

Use terminology consistently within the same lesson, interface, or
conversation. Choose **στοιχείο** for ordinary examination and **τεκμήριο**
when the context specifically calls for stronger evidential meaning.

## 5. Style Rules

- Prefer short sentences.
- Avoid abstract academic Greek when simpler Greek works.
- Avoid reproducing English syntax directly.
- Preserve constitutional meaning exactly.
- Use the second person naturally.
- Do not over-soften difficult truths.
- Prefer concrete verbs over heavy nominal phrasing.
- Keep uncertainty explicit when certainty is unavailable.
- Preserve learner freedom, judgment, and responsibility.

A natural Greek sentence may differ in structure from the English source. It
must not differ in constitutional meaning.

## 6. Slogan

**Χωρίς ψέματα. Χωρίς συντομεύσεις. Σκέψου μόνος σου.**

## 7. Governing Rule

**Language changes. Constitution does not.**
