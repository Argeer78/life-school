# Purpose

The Engine Verification Protocol defines how Life School verifies that each Engine Specification is faithfully implemented.

Its purpose is to ensure that software behavior remains governed by the Constitution rather than by implementation convenience.

Verification evaluates compliance.

It does not redefine constitutional authority.

---

# Principle

Every Engine Specification shall have:

- an identifiable implementation,
- deterministic behavior,
- automated verification,
- constitutional traceability.

No engine shall be considered complete without verification.

---

# Scope

This protocol applies to:

- EN-001 Strategy Selection
- EN-002 Behavior Planning
- EN-003 Constitutional Review
- EN-004 Revision
- EN-005 Fallback

Future Engine Specifications shall follow the same protocol.

---

# Verification Inputs

Verification shall examine:

- applicable Engine Specification,
- implementation,
- automated tests,
- audit evidence,
- constitutional references.

---

# Verification Outputs

Verification shall produce:

- PASS
- PARTIAL
- FAIL

and include:

- compliance summary,
- evidence,
- identified gaps,
- recommended constitutional correction.

---

# PASS

PASS means:

- every constitutional requirement is implemented,
- implementation matches the specification,
- automated tests demonstrate expected behavior,
- no known architectural violations exist.

---

# PARTIAL

PARTIAL means:

- constitutional direction is correct,
- one or more requirements remain incomplete,
- implementation is usable but not constitutionally complete.

Partial implementations shall not be considered complete.

---

# FAIL

FAIL means:

- constitutional requirements are missing,
- implementation contradicts the specification,
- required behavior cannot be verified,
- architectural integrity is compromised.

FAIL prevents constitutional completion.

---

# Verification Procedure

For each Engine Specification:

1. Locate implementation.

2. Identify responsible files.

3. Verify every constitutional requirement.

4. Compare implementation against specification.

5. Execute automated tests.

6. Record PASS, PARTIAL, or FAIL.

7. Identify the smallest constitutional correction.

8. Repeat until all requirements pass.

---

# Required Evidence

Every verification shall include:

- implementation files,
- test evidence,
- audit findings,
- constitutional justification.

Assertions without evidence shall not satisfy verification.

---

# Determinism

Verification shall use deterministic inputs whenever possible.

Equivalent inputs shall produce equivalent verification outcomes.

Where semantic interpretation is required, deterministic verification shall be preferred before probabilistic evaluation.

---

---

# Correction Principle

When verification identifies a gap:

- the smallest constitutional correction shall be preferred,
- constitutional authority shall not be expanded unnecessarily,
- implementation shall change before the specification changes unless the specification itself is proven incorrect.

---

# Completion Criteria

An Engine Specification is constitutionally complete when:

- every requirement passes,
- automated verification passes,
- deterministic behavior is demonstrated,
- constitutional traceability is complete.

---

# Success Criteria

The protocol succeeds when:

- implementation faithfully reflects the specification,
- every engine can be independently audited,
- architectural regressions are detected early,
- constitutional integrity is preserved over time.

---

# Failure Conditions

The protocol fails if:

- verification is skipped,
- evidence is absent,
- undocumented behavior exists,
- implementation bypasses constitutional authority,
- specifications and implementation diverge without review.

---

# Constitutional Guarantee

Every Engine Specification shall be independently verifiable.

Constitutional authority shall remain traceable from purpose through implementation.

No Engine shall be considered complete without constitutional verification.
