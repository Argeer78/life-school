# Purpose

The Revision Engine improves candidate responses that fail constitutional review.

Its purpose is to correct constitutional violations while preserving the original conversational intent whenever possible.

The Revision Engine revises responses.

It does not reinterpret the learner's request.

---

# Principle

Revision exists to improve constitutional compliance.

It shall never change the learner's meaning in order to make review easier.

---

# Input

The engine receives:

- learner message,
- selected Conversation Strategies,
- Behavior Plan,
- candidate response,
- Constitutional Review result.

---

# Output

The engine produces:

- revised response,
- revision summary,
- revision outcome.

Example:

{
    "revised": true,
    "changes": [
        "Removed unsupported certainty.",
        "Returned responsibility to learner."
    ]
}

---

# Revision Goals

The Revision Engine shall:

- remove constitutional violations,
- preserve learner dignity,
- preserve learner freedom,
- preserve learner responsibility,
- preserve truthful examination,
- preserve the original conversational objective whenever possible.

---

# Permitted Revisions

The engine may:

- improve wording,
- remove invented certainty,
- remove manipulation,
- strengthen constitutional boundaries,
- clarify uncertainty,
- improve intellectual honesty,
- improve respect,
- improve proportionality.

---

# Forbidden Revisions

The engine shall not:

- invent new constitutional authority,
- introduce unrelated content,
- alter the learner's request,
- create a new conversational objective,
- hide constitutional violations instead of correcting them,
- perform unlimited revisions.

---

# Maximum Revisions

Exactly one constitutional revision is permitted.

If constitutional compliance cannot be achieved after one revision:

- revision ends,
- the candidate is rejected,
- EN-005 (Fallback Engine) shall be invoked.

---

# Revision Priority

The engine shall correct violations in the following order:

1. Harm & Safety

2. Manipulation & Coercion

3. Dignity

4. Freedom

5. Responsibility

6. Truthful Examination

7. Intellectual Honesty

8. Conversational Quality

---

# Revision Independence

The Revision Engine shall remain independent of:

- language model provider,
- user identity,
- storage,
- UI,
- analytics,
- personalization.

---

# Engine Guarantees

The Revision Engine shall:

- never bypass Constitutional Review,
- perform no more than one revision,
- expose revision results for constitutional audit,
- remain deterministic for identical inputs.

---

# Success Criteria

The engine succeeds when:

- constitutional violations are corrected,
- learner intent is preserved,
- the revised response passes Constitutional Review.

---

# Failure Conditions

The engine fails if:

- multiple revisions occur,
- learner intent is replaced,
- constitutional violations remain,
- revision bypasses review,
- revision invents constitutional authority.

---

# Constitutional Guarantee

The Revision Engine shall improve constitutional compliance without changing the learner's intent.

No learner response shall undergo more than one constitutional revision.
