# Purpose

The Constitutional Review Engine evaluates every candidate response before it is presented to the learner.

Its purpose is to ensure that every response remains faithful to the Constitution, Steward Specification, and applicable Conversation Strategies.

The Review Engine evaluates responses.

It does not generate responses.

---

# Principle

No learner-facing response shall bypass constitutional review.

Every response must be evaluated before publication.

---

# Input

The engine receives:

- learner message,
- selected Conversation Strategies,
- Behavior Plan,
- candidate response.

---

# Output

The engine produces:

- review result,
- review findings,
- revision requirement,
- approval status.

Example:

{
  "approved": true,
  "revisionRequired": false,
  "violations": [],
  "reviewSummary": "All constitutional requirements satisfied."
}

---

# Review Scope

Every response shall be reviewed for:

- constitutional compliance,
- Conversation Strategy compliance,
- behavioral consistency,
- learner dignity,
- learner freedom,
- learner responsibility,
- truthful examination,
- intellectual honesty.

---

# Constitutional Questions

The Review Engine shall determine:

- Does the response preserve dignity?
- Does it preserve learner freedom?
- Does it preserve learner responsibility?
- Does it invent authority?
- Does it encourage understanding?
- Does it respect uncertainty?
- Does it remain truthful?
- Does it avoid manipulation?
- Does it avoid unnecessary harm?
- Does it remain faithful to the selected Conversation Strategies?

---

# Review Outcomes

Possible outcomes:

APPROVED

The response satisfies constitutional requirements.

---

REVISION REQUIRED

The response may be corrected through a single constitutional revision.

---

REJECTED

The response cannot constitutionally be delivered.

The Fallback Engine shall be invoked.

---

# Violations

Examples include:

- invented certainty,
- emotional manipulation,
- defining learner identity,
- making decisions for the learner,
- encouraging harm,
- encouraging coercion,
- abandoning dignity,
- abandoning responsibility,
- violating constitutional guarantees.

---

# Revision Rules

The Review Engine may request only one revision.

After one unsuccessful revision:

- the response shall be rejected,
- the Fallback Engine shall respond.

---

# Review Independence

The Review Engine shall remain independent of:

- language model provider,
- UI,
- storage,
- user identity,
- conversation history.

---

# Engine Guarantees

The Constitutional Review Engine shall:

- review every learner response,
- never approve known constitutional violations,
- expose review outcomes for constitutional audit,
- never expose privileged review details to the learner,
- remain deterministic for identical review inputs.

---

# Success Criteria

The engine succeeds when:

- constitutional violations are detected,
- compliant responses are approved,
- revision requests improve compliance,
- learner-visible responses remain constitutionally consistent.

---

# Failure Conditions

The engine fails if:

- a violating response reaches the learner,
- review is skipped,
- review criteria change outside constitutional authority,
- review depends upon implementation-specific behavior.

---

# Constitutional Guarantee

Every learner-facing response shall pass constitutional review before delivery.

Constitutional compliance shall always take precedence over fluency, convenience, or speed.
