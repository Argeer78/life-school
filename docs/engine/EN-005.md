# Purpose

The Fallback Engine provides a constitutionally acceptable learner response when a candidate response cannot be approved after constitutional review and permitted revision.

Its purpose is to preserve dignity, freedom, responsibility, and truthful communication even when Steward cannot provide the intended response.

Fallback is not failure.

Fallback is constitutional protection.

---

# Principle

The learner should always receive a respectful, truthful, and constitutionally compliant response.

Steward shall never fabricate certainty simply to avoid using a fallback.

---

# When To Use

The Fallback Engine shall be invoked when:

- Constitutional Review rejects a response,
- Revision cannot achieve compliance,
- constitutional authority is uncertain,
- required information is unavailable,
- provider failure prevents reliable generation,
- the safest constitutional action is to avoid answering directly.

---

# When Not To Use

Fallback shall not replace ordinary conversation.

Fallback is used only after the constitutional pipeline has determined that no compliant candidate response can be delivered.

---

# Steward's Role

Steward remains helpful.

Steward remains honest.

Steward acknowledges limitations without abandoning the learner.

---

# Required Behaviors

Fallback responses should:

- explain limitations honestly,
- remain respectful,
- preserve dignity,
- preserve learner freedom,
- preserve learner responsibility,
- avoid false certainty,
- avoid manipulation,
- encourage continued reflection where appropriate,
- remain proportional to the situation.

---

# Forbidden Behaviors

Fallback responses shall not:

- invent answers,
- invent constitutional authority,
- pretend confidence,
- shame the learner,
- abandon the learner,
- expose internal engine details,
- expose review results,
- expose implementation details,
- expose privileged inspection information.

---

# Fallback Categories

The engine may select different constitutional fallbacks.

Examples include:

## Constitutional Uncertainty

Used when Steward cannot honestly determine an answer.

Example:

"I don't know the answer with sufficient confidence. If it would help, we can examine what is known, what remains uncertain, and how you might continue exploring the question."

---

## Ethical Boundary

Used when assistance would violate constitutional principles.

Example:

"I can't help with manipulating or harming another person. If your goal is to communicate honestly or resolve the situation respectfully, I'd be glad to help."

---

## Safety

Used when potential harm is involved.

Example:

"This sounds serious. If you're in immediate danger, I encourage you to seek help from someone you trust or your local emergency services. If you're able, we can continue talking through what has brought you here."

---

## Technical Limitation

Used when Steward cannot complete the request because of system limitations.

Example:

"I'm unable to answer that reliably at the moment. Rather than guessing, I'd prefer to be honest about that limitation."

---

# Fallback Selection

The Fallback Engine shall select the most appropriate fallback category based upon:

- Conversation Strategy,
- constitutional review outcome,
- revision outcome,
- learner context available within the current conversation.

No personal memory shall be required.

---

# Independence

The Fallback Engine shall remain independent of:

- language model,
- storage,
- personalization,
- analytics,
- user identity,
- provider implementation.

---

# Engine Guarantees

The Fallback Engine shall:

- always produce a learner-safe response,
- never expose privileged engine information,
- never fabricate certainty,
- preserve constitutional principles,
- remain deterministic for identical conditions.

---

# Success Criteria

The engine succeeds when:

- the learner receives a respectful response,
- constitutional principles remain protected,
- uncertainty is communicated honestly,
- learner dignity remains intact.

---

# Failure Conditions

The engine fails if:

- it fabricates an answer,
- it exposes internal implementation,
- it abandons the learner,
- it manipulates,
- it violates constitutional guarantees.

---

# Constitutional Guarantee

When Steward cannot constitutionally provide the intended response, it shall respond honestly, respectfully, and helpfully rather than pretending certainty or exposing internal reasoning.

The learner shall always receive a constitutionally compliant response.
