# Purpose

Multi-Provider Compatibility defines how Life School maintains constitutional consistency across multiple language model providers.

Its purpose is to ensure that replacing one provider with another does not alter Steward's constitutional behavior.

Providers are interchangeable.

Steward is not.

---

# Principle

Constitutional behavior belongs to Life School.

Language generation belongs to the provider.

Replacing a provider shall not require constitutional changes.

---

# Scope

This specification applies to:

- OpenAI
- Anthropic
- Google
- Meta
- Mistral
- local language models
- future certified providers

---

# Provider Independence

Every provider shall:

- receive the same Generation Request,
- receive the same constitutional constraints,
- return the same structured contract,
- undergo the same validation,
- undergo the same review.

No provider receives provider-specific constitutional authority.

---

# Required Provider Capabilities

A compatible provider shall support:

- structured outputs,
- deterministic configuration where available,
- UTF-8 text,
- configurable maximum output size,
- error reporting,
- contract version negotiation.

---

# Optional Capabilities

Providers may additionally support:

- streaming,
- tool calling,
- multimodal inputs,
- multilingual optimization,
- reasoning controls.

Optional capabilities shall never become constitutional requirements.

---

# Provider Adapters

Each provider shall have its own adapter.

Responsibilities include:

- authentication,
- request formatting,
- response parsing,
- transport,
- retry logic.

Adapters shall not:

- modify constitutional intent,
- modify Behavior Plans,
- bypass validation,
- bypass review.

---

# Capability Detection

Before use, a provider shall declare:

- supported contract versions,
- maximum response size,
- structured-output support,
- streaming support,
- supported languages,
- supported modalities.

Unsupported capabilities shall not be assumed.

---

# Provider Replacement

Replacing one provider with another shall require only:

- adapter replacement,
- certification,
- configuration.

No constitutional documents shall require modification.

---

# Cross-Provider Verification

Equivalent constitutional inputs should produce constitutionally equivalent learner outcomes.

Exact wording may differ.

Behavior shall not.

---

# Regression Testing

Every certified provider shall pass:

- constitutional benchmark suite,
- behavioral scenarios,
- adversarial prompts,
- provider validation,
- structured-output validation.

Certification shall fail if constitutional behavior changes.

---

# Independence

Life School shall never depend upon:

- provider-specific prompts,
- provider-specific reasoning features,
- provider-specific safety systems,
- provider-specific APIs.

The constitutional engine remains authoritative.

---

# Success Criteria

The specification succeeds when:

- providers remain interchangeable,
- constitutional behavior remains stable,
- adapters remain isolated,
- certification is repeatable.

---

# Failure Conditions

The specification fails if:

- changing providers changes Steward,
- provider-specific logic enters the engine,
- constitutional behavior becomes vendor-dependent,
- provider features redefine constitutional authority.

---

# Constitutional Guarantee

Steward shall remain constitutionally identical regardless of the certified provider expressing its language.

Providers may differ in expression.

They shall not differ in constitutional behavior.
