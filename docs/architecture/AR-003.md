# Purpose

The Architecture Evolution & Versioning Policy defines how the constitutional architecture of Life School evolves over time.

Its purpose is to preserve long-term architectural stability while allowing thoughtful improvement.

Evolution shall strengthen the Constitution.

It shall not replace it casually.

---

# Principle

Constitutional authority changes slowly.

Implementation changes frequently.

The closer a document is to constitutional authority, the higher the standard required for change.

# Change Review

Every architectural change shall identify:

- purpose,
- affected specifications,
- constitutional justification,
- implementation impact,
- verification impact.

Undocumented changes are prohibited.

---

---

# Deprecation

Specifications may be deprecated.

They shall not be silently removed.

Deprecated specifications shall include:

- replacement,
- reason,
- version,
- migration guidance.

---

# Freezing

Architectural milestones may be frozen.

Example:

Life School Constitutional Architecture

v1.0

Status:

Frozen

Future work continues from the frozen baseline.

---

# Backward Compatibility

Implementation should remain compatible with existing approved specifications whenever practical.

Breaking compatibility requires documented justification.

---

# Governance

No contributor, implementation, provider, or infrastructure component may unilaterally redefine constitutional authority.

Architectural evolution remains constitutionally governed.

---

# Success Criteria

The policy succeeds when:

- constitutional stability is preserved;
- architectural improvements remain possible;
- contributors understand how change occurs;
- version history remains traceable.

---

# Failure Conditions

The policy fails if:

- constitutional documents change casually;
- undocumented architectural changes occur;
- implementation becomes the source of authority;
- version history becomes ambiguous.

---

# Constitutional Guarantee

Life School shall evolve deliberately.

Every architectural change shall preserve constitutional authority, traceability, and institutional continuity.

No implementation convenience shall override the constitutional architecture.
