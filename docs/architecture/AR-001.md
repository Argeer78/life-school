# Purpose

The Constitutional Dependency Rules define the permitted relationships between the architectural layers of Life School.

Their purpose is to preserve constitutional authority, prevent circular dependencies, and ensure that implementation never governs the Constitution.

Dependencies define authority.

Authority always flows downward.

---

# Principle

Every architectural layer may depend only upon layers above it.

No layer may redefine or depend upon a lower layer.

# Circular Dependencies

Circular dependencies are prohibited.

Examples:

CS → EN

EN → CS

allowed.

---

EN → VR

VR → EN

not allowed.

Verification evaluates the Engine.

The Engine shall not depend upon Verification.

---

PB → EN

allowed.

---

EN → PB

not allowed.

The Engine governs the Provider Boundary.

The Provider Boundary shall not redefine the Engine.

---

# Constitutional Review

Whenever a new architectural family is introduced:

Its dependency position shall be explicitly documented.

Undocumented dependency relationships are prohibited.

---

# Success Criteria

The dependency rules succeed when:

- authority always flows downward;
- circular dependencies do not exist;
- every implementation traces back to constitutional authority.

---

# Failure Conditions

The dependency rules fail if:

- lower layers redefine higher layers;
- circular dependencies emerge;
- undocumented dependencies appear;
- implementation becomes the source of constitutional authority.

---

# Constitutional Guarantee

Every dependency within Life School shall preserve constitutional authority.

No architectural layer shall obtain authority from a layer beneath it.
