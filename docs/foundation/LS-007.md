# LS-007
# Proposals and Contributions

Version: Foundation Edition v0.0.1 (Draft)

Status: Foundation Draft

Derived From:
CA-001 - The Purpose of Life School

---

# Introduction

Life School recognizes that understanding may emerge from any person.

Every human being may contribute to the continued development of Life School.

Contributions are welcomed.

Acceptance is earned through Constitutional Examination.

---

# Openness

Life School encourages thoughtful contributions from all people.

No proposal shall be accepted or rejected because of the identity, position, profession, education, reputation, or technology from which it originated.

Ideas shall be examined on their own merits.

---

# Types of Contributions

Contributions may include, but are not limited to:

- questions,
- observations,
- corrections,
- research,
- educational material,
- constitutional proposals,
- editorial improvements,
- translations,
- software,
- technological improvements,
- future forms of contribution consistent with the Purpose.

---

# Proposals

A proposal is a contribution that requests a change to Life School.

Every proposal should clearly describe:

- what is proposed,
- why it is proposed,
- how it strengthens the Purpose,
- any potential concerns or limitations.

---

# Constitutional Examination

Significant proposals shall undergo Constitutional Examination before adoption.

No proposal becomes part of Life School without completing the constitutional process described in LS-006.

---

# Equality of Ideas

Life School does not rank ideas according to who proposed them.

The institution examines ideas according to their faithfulness to the Purpose.

A proposal from a child may strengthen Life School.

A proposal from a founder may be rejected.

The constitutional process treats both with equal seriousness.

---

# Human Responsibility

Technology may assist in generating, organizing, or analyzing proposals.

Human responsibility remains essential for constitutional decisions.

No technology shall possess constitutional authority.

---

# Institutional Learning

Rejected proposals are not considered failures.

They remain part of the institution's learning history.

Future generations may re-examine previous proposals when new understanding emerges.

---

# Closing Statement

Life School grows through thoughtful contributions.

It remains faithful through Constitutional Examination.

Both openness and discipline are necessary.

Neither is sufficient without the other.
