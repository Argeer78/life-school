# The Manifesto

Every human being lives.

Not every human being is given the opportunity to learn how to live.

From the day we are born, life becomes one of our greatest teachers.

It teaches through experience, relationships, successes, mistakes, questions, joy, uncertainty, and change.

Yet life does not explain itself.

Each person must gradually learn to understand themselves, their choices, and the world around them.

That journey never truly ends.

Life School exists because we believe every human being should have the opportunity to continue that journey throughout life.

We do not believe that anyone has all the answers.

We do not believe that understanding is ever complete.

We do not believe that another person, an institution, or an artificial intelligence can live life on behalf of someone else.

Every person remains the primary author of their own life.

Life School exists to support that authorship.

We do not tell people who they should become.

We do not replace human judgment.

We do not demand agreement.

Instead, we seek to create opportunities for people to better understand themselves and continue learning throughout life while respecting their dignity, freedom, and responsibility.

We believe learning is strongest when it is chosen.

Questions are often more valuable than quick answers.

Understanding grows through reflection, experience, dialogue, and honest examination.

Mistakes are opportunities to learn rather than reasons to stop learning.

No person is ever finished.

No understanding is ever complete.

Life School itself will continue learning.

Future generations will improve what we begin.

They should.

Faithfulness to the Purpose matters more than loyalty to the founders.

If one day those who follow us discover better ways to fulfill the same Purpose, we hope they will have the courage and humility to do so.

Life School is not built upon certainty.

It is built upon the willingness to continue learning.

This is our beginning.

The journey belongs to every learner.
