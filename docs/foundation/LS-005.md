# LS-005
# Constitutional Decision Making

Version: Foundation Edition v0.0.1 (Draft)

Status: Foundation Draft

Derived From:
CA-001 — The Purpose of Life School

---

# Introduction

Every decision within Life School shall exist to serve its Purpose.

Decision-making is not an exercise of authority.

It is an exercise of stewardship.

The quality of a decision shall be measured by its faithfulness to the Purpose rather than by the position, influence, or identity of the person proposing it.

---

# The Constitutional Question

Before any significant decision is made, Life School shall ask:

**Does this help Life School fulfill its Purpose?**

If the answer is clearly no, the proposal shall not proceed.

If the answer is uncertain, the proposal shall undergo further examination.

---

# Principles of Decision Making

Every constitutional decision shall strive to be:

- faithful to the Purpose,
- respectful of human dignity,
- protective of human freedom,
- supportive of human responsibility,
- open to honest examination,
- clearly documented.

No decision shall be exempt from these principles.

---

# Sources of Decisions

Ideas may originate from any source, including:

- learners,
- contributors,
- researchers,
- Stewards,
- technology,
- artificial intelligence,
- future participants.

The origin of an idea does not determine its value.

Every proposal shall be examined on its own merits.

---

# Examination Before Adoption

No significant proposal shall become part of Life School simply because it appears useful, popular, or innovative.

Every proposal shall first be examined to determine whether it strengthens the Purpose.

Only proposals that remain faithful to the Purpose shall become part of the institution.

---

# Human Responsibility

Final constitutional decisions remain a human responsibility.

Technology may assist examination, organization, research, and analysis.

It shall not assume constitutional authority.

---

# Documentation

Every constitutional decision shall record:

- the proposal,
- the reasoning,
- the constitutional examination,
- the final decision.

Institutional memory protects future learning.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Every constitutional decision remains open to future examination.

Changing a previous decision is not considered failure.

It is considered responsible stewardship when supported by deeper understanding.

---

# Closing Statement

Life School does not seek perfect decisions.

It seeks faithful decisions.

Every decision is made with the best understanding available at the time while remaining open to future improvement through honest examination.
