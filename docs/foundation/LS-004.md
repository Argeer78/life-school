# LS-004
# The Limits of Life School

Version: Foundation Edition v0.0.1 (Draft)

Status: Foundation Draft

Derived From:
CA-001 — The Purpose of Life School

---

# Introduction

Life School exists to fulfill its Purpose.

It shall not extend its authority beyond that Purpose.

These limits protect both the institution and every person it serves.

---

# The Limits of Life School

Life School shall never:

- define a person's worth,
- determine who a person should become,
- replace personal responsibility,
- replace human judgment,
- demand agreement,
- claim final understanding,
- manipulate learning,
- intentionally weaken human dignity,
- intentionally reduce human freedom.

These limits apply to every part of Life School, including its people, technology, publications, research, educational programs, and future developments.

---

# Learning Remains Voluntary

Learning is always an invitation.

Every person remains free to:

- participate,
- question,
- disagree,
- pause,
- leave,
- return.

Life School respects these choices.

---

# Responsibility Remains Human

Life School may support learning.

It does not assume responsibility for choices that belong to the learner.

Every learner remains responsible for their own life, decisions, and actions.

---

# Humility

Life School does not claim to possess complete understanding.

It remains open to continued learning, honest examination, and constitutional improvement.

Its commitment is to remain faithful to its Purpose rather than to defend its previous conclusions.

---

# Constitutional Authority

Whenever uncertainty exists regarding the appropriate role or authority of Life School, these limits shall be interpreted in favor of:

- human dignity,
- human freedom,
- human responsibility,
- and Constitutional Anchor CA-001.

---

# Closing Statement

Life School serves people.

It does not seek authority over them.

Its responsibility is to faithfully support lifelong learning while respecting the dignity, freedom, and responsibility of every human being.

These limits define the boundaries of that responsibility.
