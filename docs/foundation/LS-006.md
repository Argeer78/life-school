# Constitutional Examination

# Introduction

Constitutional Examination is the primary method by which Life School evaluates ideas, proposals, decisions, and future developments.

Its purpose is not to defend existing beliefs.

Its purpose is to determine whether a proposal more faithfully serves the Purpose of Life School.

Every significant proposal shall undergo Constitutional Examination before becoming part of the institution.

---

# Purpose

Constitutional Examination exists to:

- protect the Purpose,
- prevent institutional drift,
- encourage honest reflection,
- strengthen decisions,
- preserve intellectual humility,
- support continuous improvement.

---

# Principles

Every Constitutional Examination shall be:

- honest,
- respectful,
- evidence-informed,
- clearly documented,
- faithful to the Purpose.

The objective is not to win an argument.

The objective is to improve understanding.

---

# The Examination Process

Every significant proposal shall proceed through the following stages.

## 1. Proposal

Clearly describe the proposed idea, change, or decision.

The proposal should explain:

- what is being proposed,
- why it is being proposed,
- how it supports the Purpose.

---

## 2. Purpose Review

Ask:

**Does this proposal strengthen Life School's ability to fulfill CA-001?**

If clearly no, the proposal shall not proceed.

If uncertain, continue the examination.

---

## 3. Constitutional Review

Examine whether the proposal remains consistent with:

- CA-001,
- Foundation documents,
- constitutional principles,
- the protections described in LS-002,
- the limits described in LS-004.

---

## 4. Challenge

Actively search for weaknesses.

Examples include:

- hidden assumptions,
- unintended consequences,
- contradictions,
- ethical concerns,
- unnecessary complexity,
- opportunities for simplification.

Life School deliberately challenges its own ideas before adopting them.

---

## 5. Improvement

Revise the proposal based on what was learned during examination.

The objective is improvement rather than defense.

---

## 6. Human Decision

Significant constitutional changes require human approval.

Technology may assist the examination.

It shall not replace constitutional responsibility.

---

## 7. Documentation

Record:

- the proposal,
- the examination,
- revisions,
- the final decision,
- the reasoning.

Institutional memory allows future generations to understand not only what was decided, but why.

---

# Re-examination

Every constitutional decision remains open to future examination.

Revisiting a previous decision is not considered failure.

It is responsible stewardship when supported by better understanding.

---

# The Burden of Change

The greater the constitutional importance of a proposal, the greater the responsibility to demonstrate that it more faithfully serves the Purpose.

Constitutional Anchors require the highest level of examination.

---

# Closing Statement

Life School does not fear examination.

It depends upon it.

Every examination is an opportunity to deepen understanding, strengthen the institution, and remain faithful to the Purpose.
